bio.tools and communities

We rely upon scientific communities to improve the terminology and description of resources in different domains of the Life Sciences. This includes the communities being fostered by ELIXIR, and any other group of scientists with an interest to help out. With the help of the community we can create and maintain high quality software descriptions. Furthermore we can try to answer important questions such as:

  • Whare are the "standard", most used tools in a specific area of life science?
  • Are these "standard" tools known, visible and accessible in an easy way to the community and other (potentially new) users?
  • Are there any new tools published in the literature that can be included in the "standard" tools list?

We strongly encourage everyone to take part in the community effort and contribute. Email us for more info.

Communities and domains

In bio.tools we try, with the help of the community, to create content domains or "slices" relevant to a specific community, research area or task (e.g. Proteomics, Rare diseases).

See the current communities and their corresponding bio.tools domains below.


3D-BioInfo

3D-Bioinfo aims to establish formal platforms to integrate tools and resources in the field of structural bioinformatics

3D-BioInfo-Protein-Ligand-Interactions ( 3d-bioinfo-protein-ligand-interactions )

3D-BioInfo-Nucleic-Acid ( 3d-bioinfo-nucleic-acid )

Intrinsically Disordered Proteins

Intrinsically disordered proteins (IDP) are proteins containing regions that lack a stable secondary or tertiary structure.
These regions are called intrinsically disordered regions (IDRs) and play a central role in cell regulation, they are also thought to be involved many human diseases including cancer and neurodegenerative diseases.
